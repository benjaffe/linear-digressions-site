What is Linear Digressions?

We're producing this podcast because machine learning is exciting!

Machine learning means looking for patterns in large datasets with statistical and computational methods, and then making predictions or solving problems. It's helped us accomplish goals that were out of reach even a few years ago.

Katie Malone

An experimental physicist by training, Katie first got interested in machine learning by using it to search for new particles like the Higgs boson. Nobody should need a PhD in physics to play with machine learning, so she built an introductory class at Udacity.

While writing that course, she found far more cool applications and exercises than she could include. Linear Digressions is Katie's way of sharing those stories. When not discovering new particles or teaching, she’s usually trail running or bribing her deskmate’s dog with treats.

Ben Jaffe

Ben is a web developer and educator. He also firmly believes that the field of Data Science will become integral to our discovery and exploration of our world. It's an incredible field, and is definitely only getting bigger.

He looks forward to recording Linear Digressions because it's often the first time he hears about the episode's topic. Ben is also a musician, grows vegetables, and cohosts GeekSpeak, a radio show and podcast about science and technology.
